# Networking Playbook — who to reach out to

**#1 rule:** the people most likely to reply are **1–4 years out of school**, ideally **Rutgers alumni**, in the *exact team* you want. Not VPs, not recruiters first. Engineers love talking to a strong undergrad who already ships.

## Warm network — mine these first (you already have credibility)
1. **NYL TDAV team** — manager + senior engineers. Return-offer lever *and* intro path to other insurance/finance AI teams. Ask each: "who else should I talk to?"
2. **Lykke founders** — founders know founders; ask for intros to YC-network founding-engineer roles.
3. **Prof. Jingjin Yu (robotics)** & **Prof. Kelvin Kwan (comp bio)** — for research-eng + comp-bio paths, a professor's intro beats any cold email. Ask: "Do you know anyone in industry doing X I could chat with?"
4. **Samaritan Scout** founders/leads — startup references.
5. **Road to Silicon Valley (Cohort 6)** mentors + alumni — built for SV intros; use for founding-eng/AI-startup paths.
6. **Quant Finance Club** alumni now at trading firms — best channel for quant-dev referrals.
7. **Rutgers Economics Labs** gov partners (NJDOL, HUD, Census) — bridge to Booz Allen / Mathematica / Palantir public-sector.

## Cold (but targeted) outreach — how to find the right person
- **LinkedIn Alumni tool:** Rutgers page → "Alumni" → filter by company + "Software" → people 1–3 yrs out. That's your list.
- Filter for **"Founding Engineer," "Applied AI," "Forward Deployed," "Research Engineer"** titles directly.
- Startups: founders/early engineers on **YC Work-at-a-Startup** and **a16z/General Catalyst** boards list themselves as the contact.

## Coffee-chat template (15-second read, high reply rate)
> Hi [Name] — Rutgers CS/Math '27, currently building AI agents at New York Life and as a founding eng at an AI startup. I saw you're on [team] at [firm] working on [specific thing] — that's exactly the kind of [applied AI / infra / quant] work I want to do. Could I grab 15 min to hear how you got there and what the team values? Happy to work around your schedule.

Why it works: leads with *builder* credibility, names a specific team, asks for time not a job, and is short.
